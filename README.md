# Lusk

Here's every room, district by district:

**THE HARBOUR**
- Harbour Gate — main land entrance to the harbour
- Harbour Promenade — the bay walkway, the city's public face
- East Quay — merchant fleet, customs, cargo
- East Quay Far End — lighthouse, open sea view
- Lighthouse Top — keeper's room, extraordinary views
- West Quay — fishing fleet, local shrine
- Old Pier — ancient timber pier, a place to sit and look
- Fish Market — loud, wet, fast-moving
- Customs House — inspection bays, tariff boards
- Customs House Upper — administrative floor, manifests and maps
- Chandler's Row — marine suppliers, charts, rope
- The Bronze Anchor Tavern — sailors' tavern, the classic spawn
- Bronze Anchor Upper Rooms — inn corridor, harbour view
- Lower Docks — waterline beneath the promenade, dark and quiet
- Lower Docks East — beneath the East Quay, unmarked boats
- Lower Docks West — floods at high tide, something in the water

**THE OLD QUARTER**
- Salters' Lane — southern entrance, old fishing families
- Copper Lane — the oldest street, lanterns, layered buildings
- Tanners' Yard — hides and leather, forceful smell
- Canal Steps — greenish stone, slow water, canal boats
- Canal Dock — waterline, sleeping taxi operator
- The Narrow — sideways-only passage, glass beads
- The Broken Wheel — ancient drinking house, cartwheel in the wall
- Old Wall Gate — carved gatehouse, fresh flowers nobody claims
- Gnomon Alley — unmarked, more worn than it should be
- The Order of Gnomes — the grotto, mismatched shrines, candlelight
- Copper Lane Bridge — second-floor crossing, pot plants
- Gatehouse Top — wind, worn carvings, city panorama

**CARNABY MARKET**
- Carnaby Approach — old quarter giving way to commerce
- Carnaby Market Square — irregular open heart of the market
- Money Changers' Row — booths, scales, motes and foreign coin
- Silk Market — vaulted iron roof, impossible colours
- Silk Market Gallery — upper level, locked glass cases
- Market Gallery Bridge — above the square, swaying slightly
- Spice Colonnade — overwhelming, unnamed packets
- Provisions Yard — back end of the market, cats on grain sacks
- Market Cellars — cold storage, connects to the sewers

**TEMPLE DISTRICT**
- Temple Row West End — transition from market to solemn
- Temple Row — the main avenue, competing faiths
- The Unnamed Temple Anteroom — plain white, silent waiting
- Temple Row East End — smaller shrines, food cart
- The Wayfarer's Garden — ravens, tiles, improbably green
- Scholars' Alley — books, noticeboards, disputes about translation
- Archive Reading Room — reader's passes, ink-stained librarian
- The Red Temple Steps — abundant god, dizzying incense

**THE RESIDENCY**
- Residency Gate — diplomatic courtesy, noted arrivals
- Embassy Row — disagreeing architectural styles, curtained windows
- The Foreign Quarter — lived-in, foreign smells and music
- Residency West Lane — quieter, steam from a laundry
- Residency East Lane — blurs into the Temple District, small fountain
- The Cliff Path — rough grass, sea wind, city falling away
- Cliff Overlook — bare rock, the whole island behind you, a cairn

**GOVERNMENT DISTRICT**
- Government District Approach — civic architecture, serious noise
- Guildhall Street — guild buildings arguing with each other
- The Mercantile Baths — steam, cedar, business in private
- Guildhall Upper Bridge — framed charters, guild clerk in a hurry

**THE HEIGHTS**
- Heights Gate — guards watching without stopping you
- Civic Plaza — white stone, worn statue, long view south
- Records Tower Base — scrolls, professional slowness
- Records Tower Top — finest views in Lusk, four islands visible
- Civic Plaza Upper Terrace — empty today, heavy doors behind
- Court of Arbiters — benches, advocates, someone arguing about a warehouse
- Treasury Steps — deliberately plain, deliberately heavy

**THE ROOFTOPS**
- Old Quarter Rooftops — laundry, cats, uncertain footing
- Washing Terrace — lines at head height, apologise to a sheet
- Bridge of Seven Arches — high, wide, pastry seller, musicians
- Market Rooftops — skylights, commercial, gap view into the square
- Guildhall Upper Bridge — also connects the rooftop network north

**THE SEWERS & UNDERWORKS**
- Sewer Entrance — ancient stone, narrow walkway, something moves
- Sewer Junction — inhabited once, grating light, scratched maps
- Sewer East Branch — wet walls, alcoves, a sealed jar
- Sewer West Branch — recently cleared, boot prints both ways
- Sewer North Channel — older structure beneath, ankle-deep water
- The Old Cistern — vast, still, black lake, columns, a hum
- Cistern Far Side — sealed iron door, unreadable script, louder hum
